N-Back Task Administration

Ensure installation steps outlined in Technical Manual are completed prior to administration

N-Back Task

Administration time: approximately 8 minutes (2 minutes per block).

What we are trying to assess in this task is working memory performance, specifically the ability to monitor and update information over short periods of time. In the N‑Back task, participants must identify whether the current stimulus matches a stimulus presented a specified number of trials earlier (N trials back). Performance on the task is quantified using target accuracy, calculated as the percentage of correct responses on target trials.

The task will run for 3 blocks experimental blocks and 1 practice block, with 40 trials per block. The task will pause after each block and a message will appear. Press enter on the keyboard to continue.

General Instructions for Administering Computer Tasks

  1. Explain the instructions for each task, verbatim, to the child as described in this manual.

  2. During the practice block, watch and see if the child is executing the task correctly. If the child is making errors during the practice, point out the error promptly and instruct the child again. A second practice is required if the child is having difficulty.

  3. Once the child begins the test blocks, the most appropriate time to recap the instructions is when the task pauses after each block. Continue to recap the instructions after every block as needed.

  4. Do not leave the child unattended at any time! Sit beside the child while they are completing the computer tasks to ensure that they are focused and still following the correct instructions.

  5. Children tire easily of these tasks because they are repetitive. Always try and encourage them to complete the task. They may take breaks in between the blocks to rest.

Setting Up the Task

Materials:

  • Laptop with PsychExp setup (see Technical Manual)
  • Laptop Charger
  • Quiet Room

  1. Check the date and the time on the laptop to ensure it is accurate.

  2. Apply the following settings to turn off the background processes that will interfere with the task:

    • Turn on Airplane Mode

    • Turn off Virus & Threat Protection functions

This process must be completed every time the task is administered

  1. Plug in charger

  2. Double click on PsychExp icon

  3. Click N-Back and then Run

  4. Select 1-back spatial or 2-back spatial from the menu then click Go

  5. Enter the Participant ID (at the bottom of the screen), then click Go

  6. Click Run

    • If a dialogue box with the message “One or more of the log or output files already exist. Continue anyway?” appears, click Yes

    • If a dialogue box with the message “nback has already been run with subject ID XXX. Do you wish to continue?” appears, this indicates the data for the ID will be overwritten, ensure this is acceptable if clicking yes otherwise click no and enter a different ID

Important Controls:

Button Actions When to Use
Enter Target Stimuli When a square appears in the same spot two times in a row.
Space Non-Target Stimuli When a square appears in a new spot.
P  Pauses the task When a participant needs a break during a block or if they put down the controller, etc.
R Resumes the task When you have manually paused the task and the participant is ready to continue
Esc Exits the task When a participant refuses to finish the task after you have tried to encourage them.

Administer the Tasks to a Participant

1-Back Spatial

  1. Provide the following instructions verbally (verbatim):

    • White boxes will appear on the screen in different spots on the screen

    • Press the spacebar (point to key) when the white box appears in a new spot

    • Press enter (point to key) if the white box appears in the same spot as the last time

  2. Draw spatial sequence as you are saying:

    • So, if the box appears here (make a mark at location 12), press space bar; then here (mark location 4), press space bar, then again here (mark again at location 4), press enter.

    • Do you understand?

    • You will also see a small white box in the middle of the screen. You can just ignore that small box. It is only there to help focus your attention.

    • Do you have any questions?

    • Go as fast as you can and try not to make any mistakes.

  3. Click Run Scenario.

  4. When the task finishes, it will end automatically.

  5. In the dialogue box, press Continue, then Finish. You can close out of Presentation.

Children may easily tire of the task and require close monitoring and encouragement when breaks in the task allow for it.

2-Back Spatial

  1. Provide the following instructions verbally (verbatim):

    • White boxes will appear on the screen in different spots on the screen

    • Press the spacebar (point to key) when the white box appears in a new spot

    • Press enter (point to key) if the white box appears in the same spot as two times ago

  2. Draw spatial sequence as you are saying:

    • So, if the box appears here (make a mark at location 12), press space bar; then here (mark location 4), press space bar, then again here (mark again at location 12), press enter.

    • Do you understand?

    • You will also see a small white box in the middle of the screen. You can just ignore that small box. It is only there to help focus your attention.

    • Do you have any questions?

    • Go as fast as you can and try not to make any mistakes.

  3. Click Run Scenario.

  4. When the task finishes, it will end automatically.

  5. In the dialogue box, press Continue, then Finish. You can close out of Presentation.

Children may easily tire of the task and require close monitoring and encouragement when breaks in the task allow for it.
